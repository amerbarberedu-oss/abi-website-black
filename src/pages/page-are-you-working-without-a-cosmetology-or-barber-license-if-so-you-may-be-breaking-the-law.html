Are You Working Without a Cosmetology or Barber License? You May Be Breaking the Law

In New York State, cutting hair or providing barbering services for pay without a valid license isn't just a technicality — it's against the law. If you're working out of your home, in an unlicensed shop, or on the side without a license, here's what you need to know, and how to get properly licensed at American Barber Institute.

The Law in New York

What New York State Actually Requires

Barbering and cosmetology are separate, state-regulated professions in New York. Each has its own required training hours and its own state exam — and in both cases, you must be licensed before you can legally work for pay. This page focuses on the barbering license, the credential ABI trains you to earn.

1

A License Is Required by Law

To work as a barber for compensation anywhere in New York State, you must hold a valid barber license. The barber license is issued and regulated by the New York State Division of Licensing Services, part of the Department of State. Working for pay without it is unlicensed practice.

2

500 Hours of Approved Training

The standard path to a New York Master Barber license is 500 hours of training at a state-licensed barber school. ABI's 500-Hour Master Barber Program covers barber theory, sanitation, cutting, fading, shaving and hands-on work on real clients — the full scope the State expects.

3

Pass the NY State Board Exam

After your training hours, you sit for the New York State Board Exam — both a written and a practical component. Passing it earns you the Master Barber license, which lets you legally work as a barber and even own a barbershop and employ other barbers.

Always confirm current requirements with the official source. New York's barber and cosmetology licensing rules are published by the NYS Division of Licensing Services.

Why It Matters

Why Unlicensed Practice Is a Serious Risk

Licensing exists to protect the public — sanitation, safety and skill standards all depend on it. Working without a license puts you, your clients and your income at risk.

It's Against State Law

Providing barbering services for pay without the required New York license is unlicensed practice under state law. The state can take enforcement action, which can include orders to stop operating and other legal and financial consequences.

Fines & Legal Consequences

Unlicensed practice can result in fines and other penalties. Because rules and penalty amounts change over time, we don't quote figures here — always check the official NYS Division of Licensing Services for the current, authoritative details before you rely on them.

Career & Business Risk

Without a license you can't legally be hired at a reputable shop, can't lawfully own or operate a licensed barbershop, and put your reputation and livelihood at risk. A license opens doors instead of closing them.

Common Questions

Working Without a License in New York — FAQ

Is it illegal to cut hair for money without a license in New York?

Yes. In New York State, you must hold a valid barber license to provide barbering services for compensation. Doing so without a license is considered unlicensed practice and can lead to enforcement action. Always confirm the current rules with the NYS Division of Licensing Services.

What license do I need to work as a barber in New York?

You need a New York Master Barber license, which is issued and regulated by the New York State Division of Licensing Services. The standard path is 500 hours of training at a state-licensed barber school, followed by passing the New York State Board Exam.

How long does it take to get licensed?

ABI's 500-Hour Master Barber Program takes about four months full-time (roughly 17 weeks at 30 hours per week). A weekend schedule takes about 27 weeks. After completing your hours you take the NY State Board Exam to earn your license.

How much does it cost, and are there payment plans?

Tuition is $5,600 for the morning schedule and $4,600 for the afternoon or weekend schedules. Every program includes a weekly payment plan so you can pay as you attend, with plans starting as low as $150 per week. Veterans and ACCES-VR funding may also help.

I've been cutting hair for years without a license — can I still enroll?

Absolutely. Many students come to ABI with real skills but no license. Enrolling in the 500-Hour Master Barber Program lets you build on what you know, complete the state-required training and pass the State Board Exam so you can work legally.

Where can I verify New York's official licensing rules?

The authoritative source is the New York State Division of Licensing Services (part of the Department of State). You can find current barber and cosmetology licensing requirements, applications and rules at dos.ny.gov.
