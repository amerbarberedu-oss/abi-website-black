The Law in New York

What New York State Actually Requires

Barbering and cosmetology are separate, state-regulated professions in New York. Each has its own required training hours and its own state exam — and in both cases, you must be licensed before you can legally work for pay. This page focuses on the barbering license, the credential ABI trains you to earn.

1

A License Is Required by Law

To work as a barber for compensation anywhere in New York State, you must hold a valid barber license. The barber license is issued and regulated by the New York State Division of Licensing Services, part of the Department of State. Working for pay without it is unlicensed practice.

2

500 Hours of Approved Training

The standard path to a New York Master Barber license is 500 hours of training at a state-licensed barber school. ABI's 500-Hour Master Barber Program covers barber theory, sanitation, cutting, fading, shaving and hands-on work on real clients — the full scope the State expects.

3

Pass the NY State Board Exam

After your training hours, you sit for the New York State Board Exam — both a written and a practical component. Passing it earns you the Master Barber license, which lets you legally work as a barber and even own a barbershop and employ other barbers.

Always confirm current requirements with the official source. New York's barber and cosmetology licensing rules are published by the NYS Division of Licensing Services.
