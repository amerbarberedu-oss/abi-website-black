About ACCESS-VR

Your Disability Shouldn't Block Your Career

ACCESS-VR provides services to qualified individuals who have a disability that interferes with their employment — getting a job, keeping a job, or advancing in one. To this end, ACCESS-VR will pay the costs of education for its qualified clients, including tuition, related fees, and required textbooks, paid directly to the American Barber Institute.

To Apply, You Must Be:

  • A person with a disability that impacts your ability to work
  • Able to work in the United States after completing your education
  • Currently living in New York State
  • At least 14 years old
  • Able to participate in vocational rehabilitation services

ACCESS-VR Services Include

  • Vocational Counseling & Guidance
  • Assessments & Evaluations
  • Rehabilitation Technology
  • Special Transportation
  • Adaptive Driver Training
  • Work Readiness
  • Training, Tuition & Textbooks
  • Tutor / Reader / Note Taker
  • Youth Services
  • Physical & Mental Restoration
  • Job Development & Placement
  • Work Try-Out & On-the-Job Training
  • Job Coaching
  • Occupational Tools & Equipment
  • Self-Employment Support
  • Occupational & Business Licenses

Applications are available in English, Chinese, Haitian-Creole, Italian, Russian, and Spanish.

The Manhattan ACCESS-VR district office is a short distance from ABI at 116 West 32nd Street, Fifth Floor, NY, NY 10001. Full information on programs, services, applications, and locations: acces.nysed.gov/vr.

Financial Assistance Is Available

If you qualify, ACCESS-VR will bear the program expenses — tuition, tools, and books — and pay the American Barber Institute directly. We'll help you navigate the application.
