Your Benefits

VA Education Benefits Accepted at ABI

ABI is part of a VA-approved job training program under Title 38 USC § 3676, and benefits may extend to qualifying dependents. These are the benefit chapters our students use.

1

Post-9/11 GI Bill® — Ch. 33

The most widely used benefit for veterans with qualifying service after September 10, 2001. Benefits can cover all or part of your tuition at ABI.

Check your eligibility →
2

VR&E — Ch. 31

Veteran Readiness & Employment supports veterans with service-connected disabilities in training for and finding meaningful civilian work — including barbering.

Disability-focused training →
3

Montgomery GI Bill®

Veterans who contributed to the Montgomery GI Bill® can apply those education benefits toward Master Barber training at ABI.

Ask about MGIB →
4

DEA — Ch. 35

Dependents' Educational Assistance extends education benefits to qualifying spouses and children of certain disabled or deceased veterans.

Common Questions

Veteran Student FAQs

What documents do I need to use my benefits?

Veterans present a certificate of eligibility and discharge papers (Form DD-214). Our finance department handles the enrollment certification process with the VA — call (212) 290-2289 and we'll walk you through it.

How do I confirm my eligibility?

Reach out to your VA Representative for guidance on education benefits eligibility, and visit the New York State Bureau of Veterans Education for state-level information. Our admissions team can help you get started.

Do benefits cover everything?

Benefits can cover all or part of tuition depending on your chapter and eligibility. Any remaining balance can go on ABI's weekly payment plans from $150 per week — see our financial aid page.

What if I have a service-connected disability?

VR&E (Ch. 31) is designed for you, and ABI provides accommodations and an inclusive environment. Learn more on our VA-approved job training program page.
