Financial Aid

Financial assistance for those who qualify — NYSDOL Grant, ACCESS-VR & Veterans GI Bill®. At ABI, we believe everyone should have access to quality education, regardless of their financial situation.

Three Paths to Funding

Ways To Pay For School

Looking to pursue a career in barbering but worried about the cost of education? Look no further. Contact us today to learn which option fits you.

Veterans GI Bill®

ABI's programs are approved by the New York State Division of Veterans' Affairs for the training of veterans and eligible persons under Title 38 USC § 3676. Bring your certificate of eligibility and DD-214 — we'll handle the rest.

Veterans page →

ACCESS-VR

For qualified individuals with a disability that interferes with employment, ACCESS-VR pays tuition, tools and books directly to ABI — removing the roadblocks between you and your new career.

ACCESS-VR page →

NYSDOL Grant

New York State Department of Labor grant options may cover training for eligible students. Ask our admissions team whether you qualify and how to apply.

Ask about eligibility →

Payment Plans

Pay While You Attend School

Every ABI program offers a tuition payment plan: a manageable down payment at registration, then weekly payments while you train. No need to pay everything up front.

  • 500-Hour Master BarberFrom $500 down, then weekly payments from $150–$300 depending on schedule.
  • 200-Hour Fundamentals$1,200 down, then 8 weekly payments of $300.
  • 50-Hour Refresher$750 down, $750 at the start of week two.
  • Scalp Micro-Pigmentation$700 down, then 4 weekly payments of $700 — tool kit included.

Why It's Worth It

An Investment That Pays Every Day

With over 50 years of combined experience, our instructors are experts in the field of barbering and will give you the skills and knowledge you need to succeed. Our comprehensive programs include hands-on training, classroom instruction, and opportunities to work with real clients.

Many barbers are their own boss and get paid every day. Barbering is a career-oriented skill in an essentially recession-proof vocation — and ABI graduates leave with job placement support and an alumni network across New York's shops and salons.
